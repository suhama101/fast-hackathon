Request for Proposal - Enterprise IT Support Services
Organization: Government of Pakistan - Ministry of IT
Reference: MOIT-ITSS-2026-12

1. Background and Objective
The Government of Pakistan - Ministry of IT invites qualified technology service
providers to submit proposals for enterprise IT support services across ministry
offices in Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, Peshawar, and Quetta. The selected vendor
will provide managed service desk support, endpoint management, network
monitoring, cybersecurity operations, cloud administration, and incident response
services for a period of three years.

The objective is to improve uptime, reduce incident response time, standardize
support procedures, and strengthen the security posture of government digital
services. The vendor must demonstrate experience supporting public-sector,
high-availability environments with strict confidentiality and reporting
requirements.

2. Scope of Services
The successful bidder shall provide:
- Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 helpdesk support for approximately 4,500 users.
- 24x7 network and server monitoring with monthly executive reporting.
- Endpoint patching, antivirus management, and asset inventory updates.
- Firewall, VPN, email security, and identity access support.
- Cloud infrastructure support for Microsoft Azure and AWS workloads.
- Incident response procedures for malware, unauthorized access, and data loss.
- Knowledge base development and staff training sessions.

3. Mandatory Requirements
- Bidder must be registered with SECP or an equivalent authority.
- Bidder must have at least five years of enterprise IT managed services experience.
- Bidder must provide evidence of at least three similar government or regulated-sector projects.
- Bidder must maintain ISO 27001 certification or equivalent information security accreditation.
- Bidder must provide SOC 2 Type II, ISO 20000, ITIL, or comparable service management evidence.
- Bidder must guarantee 99.9 percent service desk availability and 99.5 percent monitoring platform availability.
- Critical incidents must receive response within 30 minutes and resolution plan within 4 hours.
- Bidder must provide named project manager, service delivery manager, and cybersecurity lead.
- All staff working on ministry systems must sign confidentiality and data protection undertakings.
- Data must remain within approved ministry or government-authorized cloud environments.

4. Evaluation Criteria
Proposals will be evaluated using the following weights:
- Technical approach and service delivery model: 30 percent
- Relevant public-sector experience and past performance: 20 percent
- Security certifications and compliance controls: 20 percent
- Staffing plan and escalation model: 10 percent
- Financial proposal and value for money: 20 percent

5. Budget and Contract Value
The estimated budget range for this procurement is PKR 50M to PKR 80M for the
initial three-year contract period. The financial proposal must provide annual
service fees, one-time transition costs, optional add-ons, and applicable taxes.

6. Submission Deadline
Complete technical and financial proposals must be submitted no later than
15 December 2026 at 3:00 PM Pakistan Standard Time. Questions must be submitted
by 30 November 2026. Responses to bidder questions will be issued by
5 December 2026.

7. Required Certifications and Legal Clauses
- ISO 27001 or equivalent information security certification.
- ITIL Foundation or higher certification for service management staff.
- PEC or PTA registrations where applicable for network services.
- Compliance with Pakistan data protection, cybercrime, procurement, and public
  records requirements.
- Non-disclosure agreement and conflict-of-interest declaration.
- Tax registration, active taxpayer status, and audited financial statements.

8. Questions to Answer
1. Describe your proposed service desk operating model, ticket classification,
   escalation paths, and SLA reporting process.
2. Provide three examples of comparable enterprise IT support projects, including
   contract value, duration, client type, and measurable outcomes.
3. Explain your cybersecurity monitoring, incident response, and evidence
   preservation procedures.
4. Describe how your team will transition from the incumbent provider without
   disrupting ministry operations.
5. Provide a detailed pricing schedule showing recurring service fees, transition
   costs, optional tools, taxes, and discounts.

9. Submission Format
Bidders must submit a signed technical proposal, financial proposal, compliance
checklist, company profile, team CVs, certifications, project references, and
acceptance of contract terms. Proposals that omit mandatory documents may be
declared non-responsive.
