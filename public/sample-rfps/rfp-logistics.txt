Tender - Supply Chain Management Services
Organization: Pakistan Customs
Reference: PC-SCMS-2026-09

1. Background and Objective
Pakistan Customs invites tenders from experienced logistics and supply chain
management providers to support secure movement, storage, tracking, and reporting
for customs inspection materials, seized goods, bonded cargo samples, equipment,
and official records across selected border stations, dry ports, airports, and
regional warehouses.

The selected vendor will provide integrated transport planning, warehousing,
inventory control, chain-of-custody reporting, technology-enabled tracking, and
performance analytics. The solution must improve transparency, reduce delays,
and protect sensitive goods from loss, tampering, and unauthorized access.

2. Scope of Services
The successful bidder shall provide:
- Domestic freight management across designated customs locations.
- Secure warehousing with inventory control, access logs, CCTV, and fire safety.
- Barcode or RFID-based tracking for goods, samples, and equipment.
- Route planning, dispatch coordination, proof of delivery, and exception reporting.
- Cold-chain capability for temperature-sensitive samples when required.
- Reverse logistics for return, disposal, auction preparation, or transfer of goods.
- Monthly KPI reporting, dashboard access, and incident reporting.
- Dedicated account manager and escalation contact for urgent shipments.

3. Mandatory Requirements
- Bidder must be legally registered and authorized to provide logistics services in Pakistan.
- Bidder must have at least five years of supply chain management experience.
- Bidder must operate or contract secure warehouse capacity in at least three regions.
- Bidder must demonstrate GPS tracking, barcode, RFID, or equivalent shipment visibility tools.
- Bidder must provide chain-of-custody controls for sensitive goods and official records.
- Bidder must have experience with government, customs, regulated cargo, or high-value goods.
- Bidder must maintain cargo insurance, vehicle compliance, driver verification, and safety procedures.
- Bidder must provide disaster recovery and contingency plans for strikes, floods, border closures, and system outages.
- Data reports must be available to Pakistan Customs in exportable electronic format.

4. Evaluation Criteria
Technical and financial proposals will be evaluated using the following weights:
- Network coverage, fleet capability, and warehouse readiness: 25 percent
- Tracking technology, reporting dashboard, and data quality: 25 percent
- Relevant government or regulated logistics experience: 20 percent
- Security, insurance, and chain-of-custody controls: 15 percent
- Financial proposal and operational value: 15 percent

5. Budget
The estimated contract budget is PKR 65M to PKR 95M for an initial two-year
period. Pricing must include transportation rates, warehouse charges, technology
fees, insurance costs, reporting costs, and any mobilization charges.

6. Submission Deadline
Tender submissions must be delivered to the Pakistan Customs Procurement Cell by
30 November 2026 at 12:00 PM Pakistan Standard Time. Pre-bid questions must be
submitted by 10 November 2026. A pre-bid conference will be held on
15 November 2026.

7. Certifications and Compliance Clauses
- Active taxpayer status and valid company registration.
- Cargo insurance and public liability insurance.
- Security SOPs, driver verification, warehouse access control, and incident
  reporting procedures.
- Compliance with customs regulations, procurement rules, labor laws, data
  privacy obligations, and anti-corruption declarations.
- ISO 9001, ISO 28000, AEO, TAPA, or equivalent logistics/security certification
  will be considered favorably.

8. Questions to Answer
1. Describe your national logistics network, warehouse locations, fleet access,
   and ability to support border, airport, and dry port operations.
2. Explain your shipment tracking technology, chain-of-custody process, reporting
   dashboard, and data export capabilities.
3. Provide five examples of similar logistics contracts, including client type,
   contract value, duration, service scope, and performance results.
4. Describe your security controls for sensitive goods, seized materials,
   official records, and high-value cargo.
5. Provide a detailed pricing model with transport rates, warehouse fees,
   insurance, technology charges, taxes, and assumptions.

9. Submission Format
Tender packages must include technical proposal, financial proposal, company
profile, insurance certificates, warehouse details, fleet details, technology
description, project references, compliance checklist, and signed declarations.
Pakistan Customs reserves the right to reject non-compliant or incomplete bids.
